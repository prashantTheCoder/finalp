Agricultural Products

Bhagalpuriya Zardalu Aam

The Bhagalpuriya Zardalu Aam is a special variety of mango grown in the Bhagalpur region of Bihar. It is known for its distinct aroma, rich taste, juicy pulp, and golden-yellow skin. The word “Zardalu” is derived from Persian, meaning "golden.Primarily cultivated in Bhagalpur, along the banks of the Ganges.

Shahi Litchi

Shahi Litchi is a premium variety of litchi grown in Bihar, known for its sweet taste, juiciness, and unique fragrance. The word “Shahi” means royal, and this litchi truly lives up to its name. Shahi Litchi is celebrated for its distinct sweet taste and a characteristic rose-like aroma.

Magahi Paan

Magahi Paan is a famous betel leaf variety from the Magadh region of Bihar. Known for its soft texture, sweet taste, and long-lasting aroma, it’s cherished not just in Bihar but across India for its premium quality.The unique blend of ingredients and spices, combined with the technique of wrapping the paan in an edible leaf, and refreshing treat.

Mithila Makhana

Mithila Makhana refers to fox nuts or popped lotus seeds cultivated in the Mithila region of Bihar. It is a nutrient-rich, crunchy, and healthy snack that has been a part of Maithil cuisine and culture for centuries. It's a special variety of aquatic fox nut, scientifically named Euryale ferox Salisb.

Katarni Rice

Katarni Rice is a highly aromatic, short-grain rice variety grown in Bihar. The name Katarni means "dagger-shaped", referring to the shape of the paddy seed. It is cherished for its distinct aroma, delicate texture, and delicious flavor, especially when used in traditional dishes and desserts


Handicrafts Products

Madhubani Painting

Madhubani Painting, also known as Mithila Art, is a traditional folk art form originating from the Mithila region of Bihar, especially in Madhubani district. It is known for its vibrant colors, intricate patterns, and themes drawn from mythology, nature, and daily life. Traditionally created by women on mud walls and floors using twigs and natural dyes.

Sujani Embroidery Work

Sujani Embroidery is a traditional textile art form from Bihar, especially practiced in Muzaffarpur and surrounding regions.Sujani embroidery is widely used in quilts, wall hangings, bedspreads, and garments, and it received the GI tag in 2020.Mainly practiced by women artisans of rural Bihar.

Sikki Grass Craft

Sikki Grass Craft is a unique and eco-friendly handicraft from Bihar, especially popular in Madhubani, Darbhanga, and Sitamarhi districts. It uses the golden-colored Sikki grass, which is dried, softened, and skillfully woven by women artisans into beautiful items like baskets, trays, toys, idols, and ornaments.It was granted the GI tag in 2018.

Bhagalpuriya Silk

Bhagalpuriya Silk, also known as Bhagalpur Silk or Tussar Silk, is a famous handwoven silk fabric from Bhagalpur, Bihar, with a rich history of over 200 years. Known for its natural sheen, lightweight texture, and durability, it is woven from Tussar cocoons, mostly produced locally.

Applique Khatwa

Applique (Khatwa) Work of Bihar is a traditional handicraft that involves stitching decorative fabric pieces onto a base cloth to create intricate designs. Originating mainly from Patna, Nalanda, and Gaya, it features motifs inspired by nature, mythology, and daily life.

Food Products

Silao Khaja

Silao Khaja is a traditional layered sweet delicacy from Silao town in the Nalanda district of Bihar. Made from refined wheat flour, ghee, and sugar, it is known for its crispy, multi-layered texture and melt-in-the-mouth taste. What sets Silao Khaja apart is the unique climate and water of the region, which contribute to its distinct crispiness.
